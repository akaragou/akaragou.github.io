Andreas Karagounis

This year I will be completing a Master of Science in Computer Science at Brown University. My interests lie in deep learning, machine learning, data visualization and computer systems. I am presently focusing on computer vision and its applications.

I currently am working in the Serre Lab at Brown University on three research projects:
1) Training deep Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) for automating 2D and 3D segmentation of neural processes in Electron Microscopy (EM) images
2) Automating pathology by detecting tumor cells using deep CNNs and saliency maps
3) Exploration of how convolutional neural networks correlate with human vision

Projects

Automating Connectomics

Training deep Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) for automating 2D and 3D segmentation of neural processes in Electron Microscopy (EM) images. Implementations include a modified Unet and Fully Convolutional DenseNet.

View Project

Automating Pathology

Training deep CNNs to detect tumors in magnified images of histopathology. Creating saliency maps to detect tumors at the cellular level. Visaulizing learned features using Tensorflow embedding visualizer.

View Project

Modelling Human Vision using Convolutional Neural Networks

Data pipeline in TensorFlow that extracts features from each convolution and fully connected layer of a CNN and trains and tests an Support Vector Machine (SVM) on each layer. Retrieves and parses human web experimental data using SQL and Python to compute correlations between human categorizations and the categorizations generated by the trained SVMs.
